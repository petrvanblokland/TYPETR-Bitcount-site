Font family

Mobilizing the fewest number of pixels (5 × 7) capable of containing a full set of Latin 1 characters, Bitcount can be used for 8-bit screen, LED displays, or building with LEGO®.

The family has three spacing sets, five pixel shapes, five weights, roman and italic cuts, and many other features.

Five pixel shapes

The base package of Bitcount include 5 pixels shapes: Circle, Outline circle, Square, Outline square and Plus. The proportionals of the shapes are designed so they exactly match in overlap.

Five weights

Each pixel shape is availabe in five weights: Light, Book, Regular, Medium and Bold. The Regular weight pixels exactly match with the pixel grid.

Single and double

All pixel shapes and sizes make two versions of Bitcount: with stems of one pixel (Single) and with stems of two pixels where possible (Double).

Words of thanks

Petr van Blokland would like to thank Gerrit Noordzij, who initiated the concept in the ’70s that contrast in type is the factor that holds everything together, thereby countering the then-popular assumption that contrast is a luxury that needs to be removed from “simple” type whenever possible. Special gratitude goes out to his partner Claudia Mens who, since 1980, has always been a critical companion reflecting on any design question. Van Blokland also extends many thanks to his co-founding partners in Type Network—David Berlow, Sam Berlow, and Roger Black—and the Type Network production and marketing teams for making the release of Bitcount possible.
